About This Module

Why This Module Matters

Global and hybrid teams rarely share the same hours, office, or cultural norms. That gap creates real friction in how people communicate, build trust, and interpret each other's behaviour — and even straightforward tasks can become harder to coordinate when work crosses borders.

This module equips you with practical strategies for leading distributed teams effectively. Working through six structured lessons, you will move from understanding the core challenges of global teamwork to applying frameworks and tools in realistic workplace scenarios.

Designed for busy professionals, each lesson uses a mix of scenarios, interactive activities, and reflective tasks that connect directly to your own team context.

Learning Outcomes

What You Will Be Able to Do

  • LO2 Analyse workplace situations and your own professional context using relevant frameworks to identify challenges and opportunities in global team leadership.
  • LO3 Apply practical leadership and collaboration skills in realistic global team situations, including managing time zones, building team norms, and handling cultural misunderstandings.
  • LO4 Evaluate and improve your own leadership practice by using reflection, feedback and evidence to refine your approach to distributed team management.
